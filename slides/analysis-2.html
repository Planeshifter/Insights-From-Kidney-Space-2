Bivariate Analysis

Synset Correlation.

  • $X_i = \{0,1\}$ indicator of observing synset $X$ in doc $i$
  • $Y_i = \{0,1\}$ indicator of observing synset $Y$ in doc $i$

Pearson Correlation / Phi Coefficient:

  • $\rho_{X,Y} = \frac{\mathbb{E}\left[ XY \right] - \mathbb{E}\left[ X \right] \mathbb{E}\left[Y\right]}{\sqrt{\mathbb{V}\left[ X \right] \mathbb{V}\left[ Y \right]} } \implies \hat \rho_{X,Y} = \frac{n_{11} n_{00} - n_{10} n_{01}}{\sqrt{n_{1\cdot}n_{0\cdot}n_{\cdot1}n_{\cdot 0}}} $

Other possibilities: Mutual information, Jaccard similarity coefficient.

In the bivariate analysis, we are interested in the co-occurrence of synsets, i.e. their interaction fields. Specifically, we look at the Pearson correlation between binary indicator random variables $X_i$ and $Y_i$, where each of them takes the value one if the respective synset appears in document $i$ and zero otherwise. There are other approaches, for example one could use mutual information, which did not produce results as valuable as the Pearson correlation. Another promising approach would be to use the Jaccard similarity coefficient.

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

  • Characterized by progressive loss of kidney function
  • One in ten US adults has some degree of CKD

    Source: National Kidney Disease Education Program (NKDEP)

CKD is a condition characterized by a progressive loss in kidney function. The treatment of CKD is a pressing issue since today, one in ten US adults has some degree of CKD as a result from diabetes, hypertension (high blood pressure) and an ageing population.

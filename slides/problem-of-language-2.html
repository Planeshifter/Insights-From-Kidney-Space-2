Language is a challenging problem.

  • The data are unstructured
  • Language is ambiguous
  • Meaning depends often on context
Time flies like an arrow.
Fruit flies like a banana.
However, the issue is not only the verbosity of language, but the fact that is inherently ambigious. Take the following example: "Time flies like an arrow. Fruit flies like a banana". This sentence was misattributed to Groucho Marx, but in fact is frequently used to illustrate how words can have shifting syntactic functions, here from the verb to the noun.

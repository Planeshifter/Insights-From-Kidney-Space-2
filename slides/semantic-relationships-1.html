Using WordNet’s Lexical Hierarchy

We focus on the “Is-A" relationship

For a given synset, its hypernyms are the synsets that are more general

For example, if we take the doctor synset, we know that a doctor is a health-care professional. We call the health-care professional a hypernym of the doctor. On the other hand, we know that the health-professional is not necessarily a doctor, but could also be a nurse or a physical therapist, so there are other synsets which have also health-care professional as their hypernym. WordNet encodes "is-A" relationships for all its synsets, ranging from the specific to the most general.

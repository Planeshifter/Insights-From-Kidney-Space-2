Synset Relationships: Using WordNet’s Lexical Hierarchy

Contains hierarchy of concepts, ranging from very general ({entity}, {state}) to moderately abstract ({person, individual}) to very specific ({doctor, doc, physician}).

Some terminology: For a given synset, its. . .

  • hypernyms are the synsets that are more general
  • hyponyms are the synsets that are more specific

Hyponyms have an “Is-A" relationship to their hypernyms.

However, synsets in WordNet are not isolated entities, but related to each other. There are a lot of semantic relations: opposites (good / bad), cause-of (heart attack -> death), part-of (a car has wheels) etc. We focus on the taxonomy because it is the most useful in our context. For example, if we take the doctor synset, we know that a doctor is a health-care professional. We call the health-care professional a hypernym of the doctor. On the other hand, we know that the health-professional is not necessarily a doctor, but could also be a nurse or a physical therapist. Logically, doctor, nurse, and physical therapists are hyponyms of the health-care professional. WordNet encodes such "is-A" relationships for all its synsets, ranging from the specific to the most general.

Similarity Measure

$$ {\scriptstyle \hat c_i = \arg \max_{c_{1} \in C_i} \sum_{w_j \in \mathcal{W}(i)} \max_{c_{2}\in C_j} \color{lorange}{\operatorname{sim} \left( c_{1}, c_{2} \right)} } $$

Jiang-Conrath Similarity Measure:

$$ {\scriptstyle {\color{lorange}\operatorname{sim}_{JC} \left( c_1, c_2 \right)} = - IC \left( c_1 \right) - IC \left( c_2 \right) + 2 \times IC(\operatorname{lcs} \left( c_1, c_2 \right) ) } $$

Information Content of Concept $c$: $$IC \left( c \right) = - \log P(c)$$

I haven't told you yet what kind of distance measure we are using. We settled on the Jiang-Conrath similarity measure as it takes both probabilistic information as well as the taxonomy into account. Here you see the definition, which we will now disentangle. IC stands for information content. We define the information content of a concept as the negative logarithm of observing an instance of it.

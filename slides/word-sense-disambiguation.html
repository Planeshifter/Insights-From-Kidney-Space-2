Word Sense Disambiguation

For each word $w_i$, we wish to infer its correct synset $c_i$ from candidate set $C_i = \{ c : w_i \in c \}$.

Similar to approach by Fodeh et al., estimate $c_i$ as

$$ \hat c_i = \arg \max_{c_{1} \in C_i} \sum_{w_j \in \mathcal{W}(i)} \max_{c_{2}\in C_j} \operatorname{sim} \left( c_{1}, c_{2} \right) $$
$$ \hat c_i = {\color{lorange} \arg\max_{c_{1} \in C_i}} \sum_{w_j \in \mathcal{W}(i)} \max_{c_{2}\in C_j} \operatorname{sim} \left( c_{1}, c_{2} \right) $$
$$ \hat c_i = {\color{lorange} \arg \max_{c_{1} \in C_i}} \sum_{w_j \in \mathcal{W}(i)} {\color{lblue} \max_{c_{2}\in C_j} } \operatorname{sim} \left( c_{1}, c_{2} \right) $$

In order to map words to their correct synsets, we have to do word sense disambiguation, i.e. for each word $w_i$ we have to choose the correct synset out of all synsets the word in question belongs to. Consider the example sentence on the right: The word lemon has different meanings, it can refer to the fruit as well as to a defective automobile. In this example, we would like to choose the fruit synset. In order to accomplish this, we have adapted the method proposed by Fodeh et al. and estimate for each word $w_i$ its concept $c_i$ as the argument which maximizes the sum of similarities with the other synsets in its context, where we use the sentence as our context.
